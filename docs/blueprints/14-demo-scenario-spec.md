# Demo Scenario Spec

## Purpose

This spec defines the recommended end-to-end demonstration flow for the graduation project.

## Demo Goal

Show that the product is a complete workflow system, not a collection of disconnected screens.

## Recommended Demo Story

### Stage 1: Integration Readiness

1. open Integration Center
2. show connected or connectable social accounts
3. explain that connected accounts enable planning and publishing

What this proves:

- external integration exists,
- the system is ready to operate on real platforms.

### Stage 2: Create A Content Workspace

1. open Create
2. choose a source type
3. generate content for multiple platforms
4. show AI assistance and per-platform editing
5. save at least one draft

What this proves:

- source ingestion,
- AI-assisted generation,
- multi-platform adaptation,
- editing workflow.

### Stage 3: Review Draft Backlog

1. open Drafts
2. show saved draft
3. reopen it or route it toward planning

What this proves:

- workflow continuity,
- content persistence beyond a single editing session.

### Stage 4: Plan On Calendar

1. open Calendar
2. place content on a date
3. inspect the selected day in the agenda panel
4. show how the planner supports scheduling awareness

What this proves:

- planning and orchestration,
- date-centric coordination,
- interaction between creation and planning.

### Stage 5: Show Output And Recovery

1. open Published
2. show successful output history
3. open Failed
4. show failure reason and recovery options

What this proves:

- the system tracks outcomes,
- the workflow includes operational resilience.

### Stage 6: Summarize Through Analytics

1. open System Analytics
2. summarize drafts, schedule, published, failed, and activity
3. explain how the system exposes operational health

What this proves:

- the app is a system with state and measurable behavior,
- not just a content editor.

## Demo Preparation Checklist

- at least one connected account or a realistic mock state
- at least one project with generated drafts
- at least one scheduled calendar item
- at least one published item
- at least one failed item
- analytics data that reflects the above

## Narration Guidelines

During the demo, emphasize:

- lifecycle,
- orchestration,
- integration,
- monitoring,
- recovery.

Avoid emphasizing:

- pricing,
- quotas,
- plans,
- credits.

## Success Criteria

The demo works well when:

- each step logically leads to the next,
- examiners can understand the workflow without long explanation,
- the system appears cohesive and professionally scoped.
